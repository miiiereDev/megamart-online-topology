Company Profile

**MegaMart Online**

*Network Administration Capstone Project · Option 4: E-Commerce Company*

# Company Overview

**Company Name:** MegaMart Online

**Nature of Business:** Hybrid e-commerce retailer — an online department store selling groceries, electronics, household items, and apparel, integrated with a central distribution center and an online order-fulfillment operation (click-and-collect and delivery).

**Number of Employees:** 250

**Number of Departments:** 9

# Company Background

Established in 2014, MegaMart Online expanded from a traditional retail business into a major e-commerce platform. What began as a single brick-and-mortar store grew, through steady investment in online channels, into a multi-category digital marketplace serving customers across the region. As the company scaled, it added specialized divisions for procurement, logistics, and customer support to handle its expanding catalog and rising daily order volume.

With a wide product range and several interdependent divisions, MegaMart Online operates a complex internal organization that demands a well-structured, secure, and highly available network infrastructure to keep its online store, distribution center, and support teams running smoothly around the clock. The company is now investing in a properly segmented enterprise network to replace its outgrown flat network, protect customer and payment data, and provide the reliability and scalability its growth requires.

# Mission

To be a one-stop online shop that meets every customer’s needs with quality products, fair prices, and dependable service, delivered through fast, reliable, and secure digital systems.

# Vision

To lead the future of retail by seamlessly connecting online and offline shopping through reliable, innovative, and trusted technology.

# Organizational Structure

MegaMart Online is organized into nine departments. Executive leadership sets strategy, while the operational, financial, technical, and customer-facing divisions carry out day-to-day business. The Information Technology department is responsible for the enterprise network that underpins all other functions. The structure below also forms the basis for the network’s segmentation (one VLAN per department).
